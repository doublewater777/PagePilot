# Assumptions

## Riskiest Assumption

Enough first-time users arrive with computer-side books or OPDS feeds that source selection reduces import friction instead of adding choice friction.

## Supporting Assumptions

- The existing Wi-Fi and OPDS flows are familiar to current PagePilot users.
- Users understand the source names without a separate tutorial.
- Returning automatically after the first successful import is clearer than asking users to close the source manually.

## What Would Falsify This

- Users hesitate at source selection and fail to start an import.
- Wi-Fi or OPDS imports commonly finish without returning to onboarding.
- Support feedback shows users expected cross-device sync rather than one-time import.

## Existing Evidence

- PagePilot already ships Wi-Fi transfer and OPDS browsing in Bookshelf.
- The onboarding goal is explicitly to open a user-selected Publication quickly.
